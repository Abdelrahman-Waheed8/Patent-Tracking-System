1. Field of the Invention

    "The present disclosure relates to power management systems in resource-constrained environments, particularly directed toward optimizing power consumption in IoT (Internet of Things) devices by leveraging predictive behavioral modeling."

2. Background / Problem Statement

    "Modern IoT devices are limited by battery capacity. Current state-of-the-art power saving techniques rely on static timeouts that often fail to balance system responsiveness and energy efficiency, leading to unnecessary power drain during periods of inactivity."

3. Summary of the Invention

    "The present disclosure provides a method for dynamic power management. By employing a machine learning classification model, the system analyzes historical user activity patterns. It proactively transitions hardware modules (such as radio transceivers and GPS sensors) into deep-sleep modes based on the predicted probability of user interaction, achieving significant energy savings without compromising user experience."

4. Technical Implementation Details

    "The system comprises a data collection module, a processing engine for pattern recognition, and a hardware control interface. The predictive model is trained on a rolling window of user telemetry, utilizing a lightweight neural network architecture optimized for embedded execution."
